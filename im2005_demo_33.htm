1. Phase Velocity

In general, a wave propagating in +z direction can be described by a wave function: \[\Psi(t,z) = g(\omega t-\beta z)\]

When we look at a wave, we would observe the constant phase and view it as the phase velocity. \[\omega t-\beta z = \text{const.}\] \[\omega -\beta \frac{dz}{dt} = 0\Rightarrow v_p = \frac{dz}{dt} = \frac{\omega}{\beta}\]
