Phase Velocity and Group Velocity

Editor: Jia-Ren Joshua Nieh, Zhong-Han Jimmy Wu
Advisor: Professor Jean-Fu Jeff Kiang

1. Phase Velocity

In general, a wave propagating in +z direction can be described by a wave function: \[\Psi(t,z) = g(\omega t-\beta z)\] When we look at a wave, we would observe the constant phase and sense it moving at the phase velocity. \[\omega t-\beta z = \text{const.}\] \[\omega -\beta \frac{dz}{dt} = 0\Rightarrow v_p = \frac{dz}{dt} = \frac{\omega}{\beta}\]

2. Group Velocity

In the figure below, when the two waves align, we would sense it moving at the group velocity. \[\begin{cases} \lambda _A + \Delta z = v_{pA}\Delta t\\ \lambda _B + \Delta z = v_{pB}\Delta t \end{cases}\Rightarrow\Delta t = \frac{\lambda_A - \lambda_B}{v_{pA} - v_{pB}},~\Delta z = \frac{\lambda_A v_{pB} - \lambda_B v_{pA}}{v_{pA} - v_{pB}}\] \[\Rightarrow v_g = \frac{\Delta z}{\Delta t} = \frac{\lambda_A v_{pB} - \lambda_B v_{pA}}{\lambda_A - \lambda_B} = \frac{\omega_B - \omega_A}{\beta_B - \beta_A}\] In the narrow-band case, \[v_g = \frac{d\omega}{d\beta}\]

3. xample: Parallel-Plate Metallic Waveguide

Graph \(\omega\)-\(\beta_z\) for a parallel-plate metallic waveguide. The slope of the graph at a given \(\beta_z\) is the group velocity \(=\frac{d\omega}{d\beta_z}\) and the slope of the line passing origin at a given \(\beta\) is the phase velocity \(=\frac{\omega}{\beta_z}\)
